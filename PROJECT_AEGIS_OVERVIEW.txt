====================================================================
1) MISSION AND PHILOSOPHY
====================================================================
Aegis is a modular, institutional-grade decision and execution stack for
systematic trading. Its mission is to produce high-quality trade intents,
filter them through strict risk and portfolio governance, execute them
reliably, and learn from outcomes through repeatable research and model
promotion loops.

Aegis is intentionally designed to be:
- Deterministic first, adaptive second.
- Config-driven over hard-coded.
- Auditable over opaque.
- Incremental over monolithic.
- Safe by default under uncertainty.

Primary objective:
- Convert market and strategy signals into governed, explainable, and
  executable actions while preserving capital and improving decision quality
  over time.

Non-goals:
- Aegis is not a discretionary click-trading UI.
- Aegis is not a black-box "auto-profit" engine.
- Aegis is not tied to a single broker, data vendor, or strategy style.

====================================================================
2) SYSTEM PRINCIPLES
====================================================================
1. Separation of concerns
   Scoring, alpha generation, risk, allocation, portfolio accounting,
   execution, and research are distinct modules with explicit contracts.

2. Single source of truth
   Configuration and state transitions should be explicit and versioned.

3. Explainability at every stage
   Every accepted/rejected trade should carry rationale and provenance.

4. Safety gates before speed
   Risk and capital constraints take precedence over opportunity capture.

5. Progressive enhancement
   Deterministic baseline behavior must function even when advanced models,
   external services, or optional components are unavailable.

6. Operational resilience
   Retries, idempotent actions, and clean failure modes are mandatory.

7. Testability and repeatability
   Unit tests, integration tests, and replay tests are first-class artifacts.

3) HIGH-LEVEL ARCHITECTURE
====================================================================
Top-level package: aegis/

Core domains:
- scoring/       -> Trade scoring hub and component scorers
- alpha/         -> Prediction + calibration + trade intent generation
- risk/          -> Environment, trade, position, and portfolio gates
- portfolio/     -> Position state, exposure, attribution, reconciliation
- execution/     -> Order construction, submission, retry, fill handling
- marketplace/   -> Strategy registration and lifecycle surface
- allocation/    -> Capital sizing and trade quantity assignment
- meta/          -> Meta-model synthesis layer
- common/        -> Shared types, config, and logging primitives
- config/        -> YAML config surfaces for scoring/risk/portfolio/execution

ASCII architecture diagram:

  [Strategies/Signals]
          |
          v
  [Scoring Hub] ---> [Meta-Model]
          |
          v
      [Alpha Hub]
          |
          v
      [Risk Engine] ----> (Rejected trades + reasons)
          |
          v
 [Capital Allocation AI]
          |
          v
   [Execution Engine] <----> [Broker Adapter(s)]
          |
          v
   [Portfolio Manager] ----> [Persistence/Attribution/Reconciliation]
          |
          v
 [Research + Promotion Feedback Loop]

====================================================================
4) END-TO-END DATA FLOW
====================================================================
Pipeline sequence:
1. Candidate trades enter from strategy layer.
2. Scoring Hub computes component scores and weighted composite score.
3. Meta-model augments or validates aggregate confidence.
4. Alpha Hub transforms scored opportunities into normalized trade intents.
5. Risk Engine evaluates hard/soft constraints and rejects unsafe intents.
6. Allocation Engine assigns capital and quantity under policy budgets.
7. Execution Engine builds orders, submits with retry policy, tracks fills.
8. Portfolio Manager updates positions/cash/exposure and stores snapshots.
9. Attribution and research subsystems analyze outcomes and feed promotion.

ASCII flow with control gates:

  candidates
      |
      v
  score() ---- if below threshold --> drop
      |
      v
  alpha_intent()
      |
      v
  risk_check() ---- if blocked ------> reject_log
      |
      v
  allocate_qty()
      |
      v
  execute_order() ---- if failed ----> retry_or_cancel
      |
      v
  apply_fill()
      |
      v
  snapshot + attribution + research_store

5) MODULE REFERENCE
====================================================================

5.1 SCORING HUB
Purpose:
- Produce robust, explainable trade quality scores from multiple independent
  evidence channels.

Core pieces:
- scoring/engine.py
- scoring/weighting.py
- scoring/explainable.py
- scoring/meta.py
- scoring/modules/*

Component modules (current baseline):
- technical
- order_flow
- squeeze
- ml
- regime
- macro
- liquidity
- sentiment

Contract:
- Input: trade candidate + feature snapshot + regime snapshot
- Output: component_scores, weight map, composite probability score,
  confidence class, rationale list, optional meta score.

Operational notes:
- Weighting is regime-aware.
- Scoring remains deterministic by default.
- Confidence classes are policy boundaries for downstream gates.

5.2 ALPHA HUB
Purpose:
- Convert scored opportunities into executable trade intents.

Core pieces:
- alpha/hub.py
- alpha/prediction_service.py
- alpha/calibration_service.py
- alpha/model_registry.py

Responsibilities:
- Gather prediction outputs.
- Calibrate probability.
- Determine directional side.
- Emit normalized intent payload with metadata.

Expected intent fields:
- symbol, side, entry, stop, target
- probability, expected_return, holding_time
- alpha_metadata (model/version/registry details)

5.3 RISK ENGINE
Purpose:
- Apply mandatory safety checks before capital is committed.

Core piece:
- risk/engine.py

Gate categories:
- Environment gates (market state, halt conditions, macro kill-switch)
- Trade-level gates (symbol/side/qty sanity)
- Position-level gates (max position size, concentration)
- Portfolio-level gates (gross/net exposure, heat, drawdown policy)

Outputs:
- Approved intents
- Rejected intents with explicit reason list and risk flags

5.4 PORTFOLIO MANAGER
Purpose:
- Maintain system-of-record state for positions and cash.

Core pieces:
- portfolio/manager.py
- portfolio/positions.py
- portfolio/exposures.py
- portfolio/reconciliation.py
- portfolio/attribution.py
- portfolio/allocation.py
- portfolio/persistence.py

Responsibilities:
- Apply fills to internal state.
- Reconcile with broker state.
- Calculate gross/net and symbol exposures.
- Support attribution and snapshot persistence.

State contract:
- Positions keyed by symbol with qty and avg price.
- Cash tracked as post-trade ledger value.
- Snapshot is serializable and testable.

5.5 EXECUTION ENGINE
Purpose:
- Translate intent into broker-compatible orders and manage submission cycle.

Core pieces:
- execution/engine.py
- execution/order_builder.py
- execution/retry_policy.py
- execution/fill_handler.py
- execution/metrics.py
- execution/broker_interface.py
- execution/paper_broker.py
- execution/live_broker.py

Responsibilities:
- Build order payloads.
- Submit/cancel/modify through broker adapter.
- Apply retry logic for transient failures.
- Record submission/fill metrics.
- Forward filled intents to portfolio manager.

Design principle:
- Broker adapters are pluggable; engine contract remains stable.

5.6 STRATEGY MARKETPLACE
Purpose:
- Manage strategy registration and lifecycle metadata.

Core pieces:
- marketplace/registry.py
- marketplace/onboarding.py
- marketplace/selector.py
- marketplace/runner.py
- marketplace/performance.py

Responsibilities:
- Register/unregister strategy entries.
- Keep strategy metadata and active status.
- Select active strategy set for runtime.
- Store basic strategy performance snapshots.

5.7 CAPITAL ALLOCATION AI
Purpose:
- Size trades under portfolio budget and risk policy.

Core piece:
- allocation/engine.py

Policy goals:
- Respect global and strategy-level budgets.
- Bound quantity by max position and liquidity context.
- Support deterministic baseline sizing and future advanced methods.

Default implementation stance:
- Deterministic sizing based on available cash and configured fractions.
- Extendable to fractional Kelly and uncertainty-adjusted sizing.

5.8 META-MODEL ENGINE
Purpose:
- Synthesize component-level evidence into a secondary aggregate score.

Core piece:
- meta/engine.py

Typical uses:
- Ensemble arbitration.
- Confidence correction.
- Regime-conditioned weighting overrides.

5.9 COMMON LAYER
Purpose:
- Shared primitives and utilities used across all domains.

Core pieces:
- common/types.py
- common/logging.py
- config/*.yaml

Responsibilities:
- Shared typed structures.
- Structured logger surface.
- Config loading conventions and defaults.

6) RESEARCH ENGINE AND LEARNING LOOP
====================================================================
Research and promotion are cross-cutting functions rather than one file.
They should consume outputs from scoring, risk decisions, fills, and
portfolio state transitions.

Research lifecycle:
1. Collect labeled decision records.
2. Evaluate strategy and model outcomes by regime.
3. Compare baseline vs candidate model or strategy variants.
4. Promote, shadow, or retire according to promotion policy.

Required datasets for institutional operation:
- Input features per decision
- Component scores and weights
- Risk outcomes (approved/rejected + reason codes)
- Execution outcomes (slippage, fills, retries)
- Portfolio impact and attribution metrics

====================================================================
7) TESTING REQUIREMENTS
====================================================================
Test pyramid:
1. Unit tests per module contract.
2. Integration tests across phase boundaries.
3. Replay/regression tests for deterministic reproducibility.
4. Operational smoke tests for package import and startup.

Mandatory scenarios:
- Score -> Alpha -> Risk rejection path
- Score -> Alpha -> Risk approval -> Allocation -> Execution fill path
- Retry then fill path
- Reconciliation mismatch detection
- Exposure limit breach rejection
- Missing/invalid inputs fail safely

CI baseline:
- Python 3.10/3.11/3.12 matrix
- lint + typing + pytest
- targeted Aegis module tests as explicit gate

====================================================================
8) PROMOTION AND GOVERNANCE RULES
====================================================================
Model/strategy promotion should be rules-based and documented.

Baseline promotion policy template:
- Candidate must pass quality and stability thresholds over evaluation window.
- Candidate must not violate risk envelope compared to baseline.
- Candidate may enter shadow mode before active promotion.
- Rollback path must be immediate and deterministic.

Change-control requirements:
- Every promoted artifact has version tag and decision record.
- Config deltas are reviewed and committed.
- Promotion events are audit logged.

9) DEVELOPMENT SEQUENCE (RECOMMENDED)
====================================================================
Phase 1: Deterministic core
- Finalize module interfaces and test contracts.
- Ensure full replay determinism.

Phase 2: Risk and execution hardening
- Expand risk policies and broker error handling.
- Add stronger observability and idempotency safeguards.

Phase 3: Research and promotion automation
- Build model/strategy scorecards and promotion workflow.

Phase 4: Adaptive intelligence
- Add uncertainty-aware weighting and calibrated meta-model upgrades.

Phase 5: Production operations
- Add runbooks, incident handling, SLA metrics, and compliance hooks.

====================================================================
10) VERSIONING RULES
====================================================================
Use semantic versioning for package-level releases.

Suggested rule:
- MAJOR: breaking contract changes
- MINOR: backward-compatible capability additions
- PATCH: bug fixes and non-breaking refinements

Every release should include:
- Changelog summary
- Config changes
- Migration notes if needed
- Updated tests and passing CI evidence

====================================================================
11) CODING STANDARDS
====================================================================
General standards:
- Keep modules cohesive and contracts explicit.
- Prefer typed interfaces and serializable payloads.
- Use deterministic defaults and pure functions where practical.
- Avoid side effects in scorers and evaluators.
- Prefer config-driven policies over hard-coded constants.

Implementation standards:
- Public APIs must be documented by docstrings and tests.
- Errors should be actionable and non-ambiguous.
- Logs should include decision context, not sensitive credentials.
- All rejection paths must include machine-readable reason codes.

12) CONTRIBUTION GUIDELINES (HUMANS + AI AGENTS)
====================================================================
Before coding:
- Read this document and module contracts.
- Verify current config and test expectations.

When adding features:
- Update module contract and tests first.
- Preserve existing interfaces unless versioned intentionally.
- Add or update decision notes for architectural changes.

When changing risk or execution behavior:
- Include replay evidence and risk-case tests.
- Document failure modes and rollback approach.

When changing scoring logic:
- Provide rationale for weight or feature changes.
- Include confidence and calibration impact notes.

PR acceptance checklist:
- Tests pass locally and in CI.
- No unreviewed config drift.
- Decision record updated for non-trivial changes.
- Documentation updated where behavior changed.

13) GLOSSARY
====================================================================
Candidate trade:
- Raw strategy output before scoring and governance.

Scored opportunity:
- Candidate with component scores, weights, and confidence class.

Trade intent:
- Normalized pre-execution object produced by Alpha Hub.

Risk decision:
- Approve/reject result with reason codes.

Allocation decision:
- Capital and quantity assignment for approved intents.

Execution result:
- Broker response plus normalized fill summary.

Portfolio snapshot:
- Serializable state of positions, cash, and exposures.

Promotion event:
- Governance action moving model/strategy between states.
